ISOLATION OF STREPTOMYCES GRISEUS FROM SOIL SAMPLES IN MAKURDI
          

ABSTRACT
This study was carried out to isolate Streptomyces griseus from soil samples in Makurdi, Benue State. Ten samples of soil were collected and analysedfor the presence of Streptomyces griseus. The microbe was isolated by cultural methods using Casein Nutrient Agar. Further biochemical tests were carried out of which some few variations were observed, however the three (3) samples all tested gram positive and microscopy observations confirmed that they were strains of Streptomyces griseus. These results have shown that its sure to source Streptomyces griseus fromMakurdi. The study recommends that further research on the isolation and identification of Streptomyces griseusshould be done with more advanced techniques such as PCR amplification which is a more accurate method than the culture method.

INTRODUCTION
1.1 Background to the Study
Streptomycesgriseus is a specie of bacteria which belongs to thegenusStreptomyces commonly found in soil. Very few strains have also been reportedly sourced from deep-sea sediments. It is a Gram positive bacterium with high GC content. Along with most other members of the group, S. griseus strains are recognised producers of antibiotics and other such commercially significant secondary metabolites. These strains are known to be producers of 32 different structural types of bioactive compounds. Streptomycin, which is the first antibiotic ever reported from a bacterium comes from strains of S. griseus. Long before now, it is has been stated that the whole genome sequence of one of its strains had been completed. (Krainsky, 1914).

The genus Streptomvces belongs to the order Actinomycetales. This bacterium is filamentous,aerobic, gram positive and dwells mainly in soil and considered as an important source for morethan half of all antibiotics(Vaurnakis, and Blander, 2014). In the same vain, it is known to synthesize many other products like extracellular enzymes and inhibitors(Mohammed, 2009) (Chater, and Merrick) (Yang and Yiang) (Kuzhadhaivelet al., 2000) (Nicole, et al., 2002) (Mohammed).

The taxonomic history of S. griseus and its phylogenetically related strains has been turbulent. S. griseus was first described in 1914 by Krainsky, who called the species Actinomycesgriseus (Krainsky, 1914). The name was later changed in 1948 by Waksman and Henrici to S. griseus. The interest in these strains is with respect to their exceptional ability to produce streptomycin, a compound which demonstrated significant bactericidal activity against organisms such as Yersinia pestis (the causative agent of plague) and Mycobacterium tuberculosis (the causative agent of tuberculosis). Streptomycin was discovered inSelman Waksman’s laboratory, although his PhD student Albert Schatz probably did most of the work on these strains of bacteria and the antibiotic they produce.(Wainwright, M. 1991)

Streptomycinis an antibiotic used to treat a number of bacterial infections, which includes tuberculosis, Mycobacterium aviumcomplex, endocarditis, brucellosis, Burkholderiainfection, plague, tularaemia, and rat bite fever. 1.2 Statement of the Research Problem
Next to penicillin, streptomycin is the most important antibiotic (Agarwal, 2006). Apart from its tremendous importance in the medical/veterinary field; it is useful even for agricultural purposes such as for the production of pesticides and other relevant substances. Hence the need to enhance the production of the antibiotic even by checking for the availability of resource locations where this organism “Streptomycesgriseus” can obtained.

It is on this note that this research work is carried out to isolate Streptomycesgriseuseven from different soil samples in Makurdi, Benue State which will create a fore knowledge of where to source for the organism whenever need arises forsufficient yield of streptomycin.

1.3 Justification of the Study
Streptomycesgriseus still remains the only organism with the exceptional ability of producing the antibiotic “Streptomycin” which is on World Health Organisation’s (WHO) list of Essential Medicines, the most effective and safe medicines needed in a health system. (WHO MLEM, 2015). Hence, there is every need to find out locations where S. griseus can be found so that should need arises for a source of soil within Makurdi, Benue or Nigeria at large as regarding streptomycin production; this work can be utilised.

1.4 Aims and Ojectives of the Study
The aim of this study is to isolate/identify S. griseus
To identify specific resource areas in Makurdi where S. griseus can be isolated.

1.5 Research Hypothesis
The following null hypotheses were tested at 0.05 alpha level of significance.
1. Drama method has no significant effect on pupils’ interest in the teaching and learning of English language in primary schools.
2. Drama method has no significant effect on pupils class size in the teaching and learning of English language in primary schools.
3. Primary school pupils ability to speak good English has no significant relationship with drama method of teaching and learning of English language in primary schools.

1.6 Significance of the Study
Information derived from the study will create a fore knowledge of where S. griseus can be sourced in Makurdi, Benue State,knowing fully well that only S. griseus has the exceptional potential of producing theantibiotic “streptomycin. The findings of this research will also aid us to be able to know and differentiate S. griseusfrom other soil dwelling microorganisms.
